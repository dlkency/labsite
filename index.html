Innovating hazard management for a resilient future.

Based at Florida International University, our lab studies how natural hazards cascade through interconnected water, infrastructure, and financial systems. The most costly disasters often emerge from complex systems, where physical hazards interact with infrastructure, institutions, communities, and financial constraints.

Our work is interdisciplinary by nature, combining environmental modeling, data science, machine learning, systems analysis, and financial risk management to develop tools that support more resilient decisions. We co-develop these tools with water resource engineers, meteorologists, industry partners, and government agencies to ensure that our research is both scientifically rigorous and practically useful.

We are currently recruiting students at all levels who are passionate about environmental risk, climate resilience, infrastructure systems, data science, and financial risk management.

Environmental risks are now major financial concerns...

 Our research develops financial strategies and data-driven models to understand how hazards affect communities and infrastructure, and to design effective risk mitigation strategies from a systems perspective.
Risk Assessment

Analyzing how environmental hazards affect interconnected natural, engineered, and financial systems.

AI Frameworks

Developing machine learning and high-resolution simulation models to predict environmental hazards and system-level impacts.

Financial Mitigation 

Designing financial strategies that help communities, utilities, and institutions manage risk and strengthen resilience.
